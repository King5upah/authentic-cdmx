Myths & truths

What you're worried about
before coming — the truth

MYTH

“Mexico City is super dangerous”

Truth: tourist areas are fine by day. At night use Uber, don't flash valuables — same as any big city. Safer with a local — I know which streets to take and which to skip.

MYTH

“Everything is spicy”

Truth: the salsa is on the side. You control it. Just say “no spicy / sin chile.”

TRUE

Don't drink the tap water

Stick to bottled water. Restaurant ice is usually purified — you'll be fine eating out.

TRUE

Tipping is expected

10–15% at restaurants. Uber and Google Translate work great. Bring some cash + a Visa/Mastercard.

TRUE

Altitude: 2,240 m (7,350 ft)

The city sits high. You may get slightly winded walking fast. Don't over-pack day one, drink water.

MYTH

“Nobody speaks my language”

Truth: basic English works in tourist zones. With me it's fully English/Spanish — ordering, taxis, haggling all handled.
